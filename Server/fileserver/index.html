The Gopher

What's a Gopher?

Gophers are small burrowing rodents that can be found throughout North America. They are known for their complex tunneling systems and can be quite the pest for gardeners and farmers. While gophers are often seen as pests, they play a crucial role in soil aeration, which can lead to healthier plants.

Why is it related to Go?

In the world of programming, the Gopher is the mascot of the Go programming language. The Go Gopher was designed by Renee French. The Gopher represents the fun, approachable, and developer-friendly nature of the Go language.

Fun Facts About Gophers

  • They have strong forelimbs that are adapted for digging.
  • Gophers can close their lips behind their front teeth, preventing dirt from entering their mouth while digging.
  • They have a keen sense of hearing, smell, and touch (but not so much sight).
  • Gophers are solitary creatures, only coming together to mate.
